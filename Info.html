Background of the study

”Voices of the youth” is the opinions, different thoughts, views, dispositions, and knowledge that is needed to be expressed by the young. Silencing the youth’s voices occurs when adults strip away teenage people's power to speak which is a factor commonly known by everyone. This occurs immediately, such as when parents order children to remain quiet or take away their communication equipment. It sometimes occurs indirectly, such as when kids want adult approval and elders do not provide it, this usually happens within a toxic household.

Adults, as a major role in repressed voices of the young, have an impact on their surroundings and the self-esteem of teens. This factor may be researched utilizing honest surveys and past studies. Using this strategy, researchers are charged with creating questionnaires that correspond with this topic in order to collect data and propose strategies to mitigate it. “The decline of student rights began with the U.S. Supreme Court’s 1985 decision in New Jersey v. T.L.O., a Fourth Amendment search and seizure case.” Hudson, D. L. The silencing of student voices - Preserving free speech in America’s schools.

Recent studies suggest that adults, specifically parents/guardians and teachers are the fundamental factors in the repressed voices of the young. Toxic households and too often school officials ignore the freedom of students to express significant beliefs, opinions, proposals, and etc. In addition to that, their voices are routinely and consistently removed from numerous discourses, often by mistake, but more often by callous intent and mindless division.


Statement of the Problem and Research Questions

The study will determine how adults suppress the voice of teens and the other sub-factors as well. We believe that suppressed voices can affect teenager’s personal growth and development because of many factors that can hinder their confidence and self-esteem. Every situation like this begins at home where every child has developed their characteristics and personalities through the family connection and environment. There are some instances that teens cannot voice out their opinions because of the phrases that adults tend to say which validates teenagers feelings. We are used to following an adult’s perspective as it has become tradition already. From the qualitative research and from the initial data from the researchers’ survey, the majority of the Teenager’s felt that their feelings are often felt that their statements are being disregarded commonly because they follow adult’s more and some have low self esteem.

This study focuses on this general question: Find among three of the most essential factors influencing youth’s voices, and how does it impede their development? In line with this are these specific questions; In comparison to other toxic settings like friends and self - esteem, how did adults come to be one of the most prevalent factors that impact youth? And to help in providing solutions to our problem we may also ask what are the most effective communication strategies for solving relationships with adults and youths in terms of coinciding and accepting each other’s opinion? All of these three questions will reveal its answers all throughout this research.


Significance of the Study

Teenagers/Students. This study proves that silencing a teenager's voice has an effect on their growth and surroundings. This will help children regain their confidence to speak up for themselves and discover how they might change their interactions with an adult to provide the groundwork for a stronger connection. They can also broaden their perspectives in order to identify and strengthen their own flaws in order to boost their self-esteem. Furthermore, teenagers would obtain effective communication and listening techniques, allowing them to not only build confidence but also overcome obstacles and achieve many possibilities.

Parents and Guardians. The outcomes of this research will indicate that it is simpler to interact with a teenager if parents and guardians take the time to listen and communicate properly. This would improve their parenting capabilities and build a closer bond with their children because youth want to express themselves and have someone listen to them. This allows parents and guardians to interact with their children while simultaneously taking care of them.

Learning Facilitators. Too frequently, school administrators have overlooked the significance of fundamental rights and freedoms. Effective communication and support for youth also lays the groundwork for teachers to genuinely listen to and respect their students. Learning facilitators will increase their teaching capabilities as a result of this study, as will their ability to handle their pupils without contempt. This will help teachers to easily relate with their students while also teaching them with the use of a proper communication method.

Other researchers. This study will contribute to the accumulation of knowledge that others may use as a resource for examining the factors that suppress the voices of the young and impact their personal growth.
